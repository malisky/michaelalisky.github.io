I met the director, Raisa, who was born in Mongolia to a Kazakh family. She completed her undergraduate studies in Egypt and has also lived in the Netherlands, UK, and Germany. After visiting Kazakhstan in 2016, she noticed a striking lack of Kazakh-language books, particularly for children, and decided to establish a publishing house.

The shortage of Kazakh books is rooted in Soviet-era policies. During this period, Almaty (then the capital) had only two Kazakh-language schools, with Russian dominating the education system. Collectivization, famines, and population transfers reduced ethnic Kazakhs to just 30% of the population. For those interested in a deeper dive into this history, this video provides an excellent overview.

However, efforts by civil society actors like Raisa coupled with government initiatives have led to a resurgence of the Kazakh language.
