June 26, 2025: Dandelion Dispatch

Below are a few photos from an overnight train ride from Shanghai to Beijing (shenanigans not included).

After arriving in Beijing, we headed straight for the Dandelion school, a non-profit educational facility serving China's domestic migrant children. Thanks to China's hukou (household registration) system, one's place of birth determines access to public services, including education, healthcare, and housing. Among China's 71 million migrant children, many recieve little or no schooling at all, and those who do manage to enroll in ordinary public schools face systematic discrimination as teachers divert time and resources toward students who are eligble for middle school entrance exams. This exclusion reflects a broader discourse positioning rural migrants as inherently inferior. Places like Dandelion school have thus become essential empowerment spaces, offering not just education but hope for breaking cycles of exclusion. Today, the school provides quality education and a nurturing environment for over 500 students from low-income migrant families.
