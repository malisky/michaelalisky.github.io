August 10, 2024: Boxing and Bazaar

For some reason, there are punching bag arcade machines randomly on the streets in Almaty. I probably saw four or five of them today. Its a pretty funny business model, really targeting the walking around looking for something to punch crowd. Maybe its more of a first date kind of thing. Im curious what you think. Realistically, its probably designed for drunk people. At 21¢ a whack, I can easily see this becoming my next vice.

Boxing is a big deal here, accounting for 24 of Kazakhstan's 75 all-time Summer Olympic medals (source), including 7 golds. The government spends (source) $372 million a year on combat sports, and some 30,000 amateur boxers actively compete (source) throughout the country. In 2011, Kazakhstan invested heavily in the Olympic boxing federation in a deal that raised significant ethical concerns (source) due to its lack of transparency. Must be all the potassium.

On an unrelated note, I had a big doner kebab yesterday (it looks normal sized in the photo, but that's just because I have been going to the gym recently).

Here's another angle for reference, featuring new friends, Tlegen and Akezhan. The drink is ayran, a mix of yogurt, salt, and water.

I also went to the Zhetysu-Semirech'ye (Жетысу-Семиречье) bazaar on the outskirts of Almaty. People sold all sorts of trinkets and antiques, and there was a strange amount of non-functioning computer equipment. I bought a 1970s Soviet pin and considered buying a kobyz (traditional Kazakh instrument), but in the end decided against it.

My taxi driver unfortunately rear-ended another car as we were leaving the bazaar, and an old apashka (апашка, the Kazakh version of babushka) stormed out of the drivers seat. Thankfully, everyone was okay, and after a bit of arguing, the drivers exchanged cash and parted ways. I saw something very similar when visiting Marcelo in Lima, and I think its worth remembering that the rule of law can be very inefficient in comparison to informal dispute resolution mechanisms.

Overall, my internship is going well, and Im meeting all sorts of interesting people. In the meantime, theres been no shortage of unexpectedly cool things at every corner. I hope everyone has a great weekend!
